There are five stages, A through E, in the SCAI SHOCK classification. Patients in stage A are at risk for cardiogenic shock but have not yet developed it. Patients in stage B have hemodynamic instability without hypoperfusion. Patients in stage C have hemodynamic instability and evidence of hypoperfusion. Patients in stage D are not responding to initial support, as evidenced by rising lactate levels, increased doses of vasoactive drugs, and potential need for additional mechanical circulatory support to maintain perfusion. Patients in stage E have refractory shock with actual or impending circulatory collapse. [^1][^2][^3]
