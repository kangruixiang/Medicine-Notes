# premature cycling is caused by high trigger sensitivity or low inspiratory time

- related: [[understanding of ventilators]]
- tags: #permanent #icu
---

<!-- premature cycling ventilator appearance b:474579760950-->

Premature cycling is when the breath terminates before patient can have enough air. This leads to inspiratory flow by patient when the machine is in expiration phase:

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20221220112552.png)

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20221217185622.png)

On the pressure curve, you'll see dip in pressure (patient inspiring causing more negative pressure). On the flow curve, you'll see a bump (more flow going in)

In pressure targeted mode, this is usually from having too high of trigger sensitivity (e.g. 50% of peak inspiratory flow). In flow targeted mode, this is usually from too short of inspiratory time.[^1][^2]
