Currently, 5 DOACs are available: dabigatran, which inhibits thrombin, and apixaban, rivaroxaban, betrixaban, and edoxaban, which inhibit factor Xa. Note that all of the generic names for factor Xa inhibitors include “x-aban.”  To determine if a patient should be considered for reversal of their DOAC, you need to (1) determine if it is a major bleed and (2) assess the anticoagulation status. Major bleeds are defined by the location (critical sites) and volume of blood loss. Anticoagulation status is based on the specific agent, time of last dose, and renal function.

This patient’s hemodynamic instability meets criteria for major bleeding, and his last dose of apixaban was the morning of his GI bleed; therefore, he is fully anticoagulated and meets criteria for reversal with andexanet alfa (choice C is correct). ==Andexanet alfa==, a recombinant variant of human factor Xa, is a ==decoy protein== that ==binds both direct factor Xa inhibitors and indirect factor Xa inhibitors== (heparin, fondaparinux), thereby preventing inhibition of endogenous factor Xa. Andexanet alfa produces a reduction in Xa activity within 2 to 5 min and has a short duration of effect (~3 h). Its high cost (approximately $25,000 and $49,500, per dose of lowand high-dose andexanet, respectively) may limit its use in clinical practice. Of note, it does not have activity against dabigatran. Prior to the Food and Drug Administration approval of andexanet alfa in 2018, ==4F-PCC== was the only reversal agent for patients with major bleeds on direct factor Xa inhibitors. 4F-PCC is a concentrate of ==factors II, VII, IX, and X==. As both andexanet alfa and 4F-PCC are associated with increased thrombosis, they should only be administered for severe bleeds and should not be administered simultaneously or sequentially. While vitamin K reverses the anticoagulation from VKAs, such as warfarin, it does not have any reversal activity for apixaban (choice B is incorrect). Likewise, protamine sulfate reverses the effects of heparin, but it does not have reversal activity for apixaban (choice D is incorrect).
