# ischemia hypoxia hypoxemia differences

- related: [[pathophysiology of hypoxia and hypoxemia]]
- tags: #permanent
---

## Definitions

- hypoxemia is decreased oxygen saturation in the blood ($SaO_2$)
- hypoxia is decreased oxygen delivery to tissues
- ischemia is decreased blood flow to tissues[^1]

## Hypoxia without hypoxemia

You can have hypoxia without hypoxemia: normal $O_2$ sat but decreased $O_2$ to the tissues. Hypoxia is oxygen delivery to tissues and rely on also cardiac output and hemoglobin concentration ([[oxygen delivery is dependent on cardiac output and oxygen content]]). Patients with heart failure or are severely anemic can have normal O2 sat but decreased delivery.
