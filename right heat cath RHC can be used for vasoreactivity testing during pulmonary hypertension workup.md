<!-- what is pulmonary vasoreactivity b:293406733101-->

==Calcium channel antagonists== have limited utility in the treatment of PAH and are reserved for patients who ==demonstrate vasoreactivity== in response to a short-acting pulmonary vasodilator, defined as a ==reduction in the mean pulmonary artery pressure by ≥10 mm Hg== and reaching an ==absolute mean pulmonary arterial pressure of ≤40 mm Hg==. Because this patient does not exhibit pulmonary vasoreactivity, treatment with the calcium channel antagonist nifedipine would not be of utility (choice D is incorrect).[^1]
