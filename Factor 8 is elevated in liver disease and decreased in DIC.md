# Factor 8 is elevated in liver disease and decreased in DIC

- related: [[Hematology]]
- tags: #permanent

---

Patients with liver disease can have similar coagulopathy labs compared to patients with DIC. In this case, one could check factor 8 level to distinguish between the two. Factor 8 is produced by endothelial cells and is released as liver cells are destroyed (elevated in liver disease). In DIC, activated protein C breaks down factor 8 (lower in DIC).[^1]

[^1]: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4087534/
