# spinal cord compression in malignancy

- related: [[Oncology]]
- tags: #literature #hemeonc
---

## Causes

<!-- spinal cord compression can happen most frequently in which cancers? b:756713008196-->

- mostly from cancer that can metastasize to bones
	- multiple myeloma, lung, breast, prostate
	- lymphoma (paraspinal mass extension)[^1]

## Symptoms and Diagnosis

<!-- spinal cord compression in malignancy presentations and imaging b:694491181747-->

- pain may precede neurologic symptoms for several weeks
- look for weakness
- bladder, bowel dysfunction (late findings)
- MRI with contrast is preferred
- CT myelography is alternative

## Treatment

<!-- spinal cord compression in malignancy treatment b:131194821336-->

- steroids to mitigate vasogenic edema
- rad onc vs surgery
