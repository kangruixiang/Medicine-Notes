# start CPET interpretation with finding peak VO2

- related: [[cardiopulmonary exercise test CPET]]
- tags: #permanent #pulmonology
---
- $V_{O_2}$ is the amount or volume of oxygen inhaled
- during exercise, as workload increases, $V_{O_2}$ should increase until it hits a threshold
- this threshold is peak $V_{O_2}$, point of highest oxygen intake
- It's a measure of aerobic capacity before anaerobic metabolism increases
- low peak $V_{O_2}$ carries poor prognosis[^1]
- normal $V_{O_2}$ means normal exam (no cardiopulmonary, neuromuscular disease), obese patient (reaching predicted volume but feeling short of breath because of deconditioning) or early disease (feeling short of breath because of disease but still reaching predicted volume)
- low $V_{O_2}$ could mean disease but could also be poor effort[^2]
