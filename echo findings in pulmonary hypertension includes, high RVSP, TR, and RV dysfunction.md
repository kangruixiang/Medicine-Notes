# echo findings in pulmonary hypertension includes, high RVSP, TR, and RV dysfunction

- related: [[pulmonary hypertension]]
- tags: #permanent
- source: [[pulmonary hypertension 1 31 2023 lecture]]
---

Major finding for pulmonary hypertension on echo includes elevated RVSP:

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20230213210018.png)

- [source](https://www.idmed.org/idaho/assets/files/AM2019/PH_PAH.pdf)

RVSP can be an unreliable finding, as it depends on TR:

$$ RVSP=4(V)^2+ RAP $$

- where $V$ is tricuspid regurgitation peak
- TR may not be detected in up to half of pulmonary hypertension patients
- Image quality is affected in patients with lung disease
- RAP is estimated and can be inaccurate
- TR velocity is independent of severity (e.g. mild regurgitation can have high peak velocity)

A better measurement is to use peak TR velocity itself:

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20230213210642.png)

- [source](https://erj.ersjournals.com/content/53/1/1801904)
- TR > 3.4 has high probability of PH
