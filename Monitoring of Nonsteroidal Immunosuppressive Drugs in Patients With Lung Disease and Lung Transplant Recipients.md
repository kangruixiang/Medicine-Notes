# Monitoring of Nonsteroidal Immunosuppressive Drugs in Patients With Lung Disease and Lung Transplant Recipients

- related: [[ILD Interstitial Lung Disease or Diffuse Parenchymal Lung Disease]], [[Pulmonary Diseases]]
- tags: #literature
- source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3610695/

---

- anti TNF-a agents
	- etanercept, adalimumab, infliximab
	- TB
		- recommend CXR, latent skin TB test prior to treatment
		- if positive, check for active TB infection prior to treatment
	- heart failure
		- avoid TNF alpha agents in NYHA III/IV heart failure patients
- calcineurin inhibitors
	- tacrolimus, cyclosporin
- methotrexate
	- screen CBC and LFT prior to initiation
	- screen alcohol and viral hepatitis prior to treatment
	- add folic acid supplementation
	- evaluate new lung symptoms
	- stop treatment if patient has persistently high LFT above baseline
- mycophenolic acid (MPA)
	- monitor weekly CBC first month of treatment, every 2 weeks 2nd and 3rd month, once a month rest of year
	- obtain blood level if GI side effects (diarrhea) develop
	- avoid live vaccines, avoid use with azathioprine
	- pause or reduce dose in GI side effects
	- stop treatment in patients with signs of PML
- hydroxychloroquine
	- eye exam once a year
