# introducer, mac, cordis can be used for resuscitation

- related: [[IV and Lines]]
- tags: #literature #icu
---

<!-- what's the difference between MAC, Cordis, Sheath and their sizes? b:1617468341609-->

- sheath/introducer is generic term for line with port to allow introducing other lines
- MAC and Cordis are both introducers. Mac introducer:

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20250806091032.png)
![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20250806084748.png)
- middle port allows for introducing other catheters (e.g. Swan)
- distal, brown port is 9 French (equivalent of 11 G: [[sizes in french and gauge]])
- in contrast, triple lumen distal port is 7 Fr
- proximal, white port is 12G
- importantly, if catheter is placed, white port becomes faster than brown port
- Cordis:

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20250806091418.png)
- Percutaneous sheath introducer (PSI) is another example:

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20250806090857.png)

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20250806090849.png)

- PSI is shorter and allows more large volume resuscitation. But it only has one side lumen
