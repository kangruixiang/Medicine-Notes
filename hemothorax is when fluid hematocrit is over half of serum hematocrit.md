# hemothorax is when fluid hematocrit is over half of serum hematocrit

- related: [[pleural effusion]]
- tags: #literature #pulmonology

<!-- hemothorax fluid studies -->

This patient's history, chest radiographs, and pleural fluid are most consistent with that of a chronic traumatic left hemothorax. The ==pleural fluid hematocrit== in acute hemothorax (<3 days) is ==typically >50% of that of the peripheral blood==, but in ==chronic hemothorax==, the ==hematocrit may be lower== due to lysis of RBCs and sequestration of fluid into the pleural space. Furthermore, the pleural fluid in a chronic hemothorax can ==share some characteristics== with ==pleural space infection== with low pH and elevated WBC count as were seen in the present case.[^1][^2]
