# PAPi is ratio of pulmonary artery pulse pressure to right atrial pressure

- related: [[cardiac hemodynamics and pressures]]
- tags: #permanent
---
<!-- what does PAPi mean and how is it calculated b:439524788687-->

pulmonary artery pulse pressure/CVP: marker of how well RV is function. Wider pulse pressure/stroke volume = bigger contractility. Lower CVP = better emptying RV. Less than 0.9 = sick

PAPi is ratio of pulmonary artery pulse pressure (pulmonary artery systolic - pulmonary artery diastolic) to right atrial pressure.[^1]

Right atrial pressure is measure of congestion. PA pulse pressure is measurement of RV stroke volume and afterload.

$$
PAPi = \frac{PASP-PADP}{RAP}
$$

[[Low PAPi is predictor of mortality in right sided heart failure]].

[^1]: https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIRCHEARTFAILURE.121.009085
