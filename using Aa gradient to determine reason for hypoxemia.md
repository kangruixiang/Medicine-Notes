# using Aa gradient to determine reason for hypoxemia

- related: [[pathophysiology of hypoxia and hypoxemia]]
- tags: #permanent
---

Aa gradient is difference between oxygen in the alveoli and oxygen diffused in the blood:

$$
Aa\space gradient= P_{AO_2} - P_{aO_2} 
$$

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/gzZuud7.jpg)

Normal Aa gradient is 10-15 mmHg.

Hypoxemia is decreased $O_2$ saturation, which is directly affected by $P_{aO_2}$ ([[alveolar gas equation calculates oxygen content of alveoli]]).

This could occur as result of either decreased available oxygen prior to oxygen exchange in the lung (low $P_{AO_2}$) or from decreased oxygen diffusion (low $P_{aO_2}$):

- decreased available oxygen prior to exchange: this will have normal $P_{AO_2}$ to $P_{aO_2}$ gradient
	- hypoventilation
	- high altitude
- decreased oxygen diffusion: this will have elevated Aa gradient:
	- fibrosis
	- low V/Q: can treat with O2
	- dead space: V/Q = infinity: can treat with O2
	- shunt: V/Q = 0: cannot treat with O2[^1]

[^1]: [[05 Hypoxia - Boards and Beyond]]
