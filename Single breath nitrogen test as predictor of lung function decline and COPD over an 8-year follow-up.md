# Single breath nitrogen test as predictor of lung function decline and COPD over an 8-year follow-up

- related: [[COPD chronic obstructive pulmonary disease]]
- tags: #literature
- source: [sciencedirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2531043722002124?via%3Dihub)

---

- single breath nitrogen test (SBN) is used to detect early small airway disease (SAD)
- there may be link between SAD and COPD progression
- this study shows SBN is associated with COPD LFT decline
- study methods
	- longitudinal, prospective study from Po River Delta in Italy
	- 8 year follow up
- result
	- slope of alveolar plateau (N2-slope) associated with FEV1 decline (7.93 ml/year per 1 unit change in slope) and also increased risk of developing COPD
	- RR 1.81 for mild and 2.78 for severe
