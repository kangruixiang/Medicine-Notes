# bronchoscopy lidocaine method and dosing

- related: [[Pulmonary Diseases]]
- tags: #permanent #pulmonology
---

## Materials Needed

- lidocaine 2% solution
- lidocaine 2% jelly (6 or 12 cc)
- 4 10 cc slip tip syringe
- 5 20 cc slip tip syringe
- 4 x 4 gauze
- atomizer device
- 2 qtips

## Details

- Total lidocaine dose can be given is 8 mg/kg
	- approximately 29 cc of a 2% solution for a 70 kg patient [^1][^2]
	- 1 cc of lidocaine is 20 mg for 2% solution
- use lidocaine 2% solution for atomizer and Jackson forceps
- for nebulizing: 5 cc nebulize with face mask[^3]
- atomizer: draw up 4-5 cc total
	- give each nostril 1 cc x2 for total of 4 cc
	- give back of throat 1 cc
	- this would give total of 5 cc lidocaine, or 100 mg
	- alternatively can give 4 cc in oropharynx if bronching through nose
- use lidocaine 2% jelly for qtips (6 or 12 cc)
- for cords and carina: use 10 cc syringe x4 for total of 12 cc
	- use 3 cc x 2 both for cords
	- use 3 cc x 2 at the carina
- **total dose: 23-29 cc**
- toxicity for over dose:
	- [[methemoglobinemia pathophysiology and causes]]
	- CNS toxicity: confusion, lightheadedness, convulsions
	- cardiovascular: arrythmias
