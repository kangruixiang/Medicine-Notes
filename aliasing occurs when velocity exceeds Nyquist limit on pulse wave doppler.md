# aliasing occurs when velocity exceeds Nyquist limit on pulse wave doppler

- related: [[pulse wave doppler measures location while continuous wave measures speed]]
- tags: #permanent
---

- aliasing occurs in pulse wave doppler mode when the velocity of the flow is higher than the Nyquist limit
- In this case, the color can appear to be on opposite side (unexpected color change)[^1]
- e.g. red becomes lighter and lighter until it switches to blue and vice versa

[^1]: [[Washington University Manual of Echocardiography]]
