# pulse wave doppler measures location while continuous wave measures speed

- related: [[cardiac ultrasound essentials]]
- tags: #permanent
---

- one cannot measure both speed and location at the same time
- pulse wave doppler measures speed at certain location but is limited by the Nyquist limit
- [[aliasing occurs when velocity exceeds Nyquist limit on pulse wave doppler]]
- continuous wave doppler measures peak velocity. Use continuous wave to measure high velocity flows[^1]

[^1]: [[Washington University Manual of Echocardiography]]
