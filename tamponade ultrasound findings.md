# tamponade ultrasound findings

- related: [[pericardial effusion with tamponade physiology]]
- tags: #literature #cardiology
---

<!-- ultrasound findings in pericardial effusion -->

Still images of the echocardiogram show a four-chamber view demonstrating a ==large anterior pericardial effusion== (Figure 1) and significant ==respirophasic variation of mitral valve inflow== (Figure 2). The ==variation in mitral== (and tricuspid) ==inflow with respiration== has the same mechanism as pulsus paradoxus.

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20251018140036.png)

<video controls src="https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/002e27ec-6efd-404c-9d81-aad65bd16846.mp4"></video>

Mitral ==inflow velocity variation== ==greater than 25%== and tricuspid inflow velocity variation greater than 40% are considered significant and thus indicate a hemodynamically significant effusion. ==RV collapse== in diastole is the echocardiographic hallmark of frank tamponade.[^1][^2]
