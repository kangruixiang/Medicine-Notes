# lactated ringer is safe for treating hypercalcemia

- related: [[Hypercalcemia]], [[fluid management in the ICU]]
- tags: #permanent
---

Both normal saline and lactated ringer can be used for hypercalcemia depending on the context:

- if patient is acidotic, avoid normal saline because it can cause more acidosis
- lactated ringer has calcium but has lower concentration and should generally be safe[^1]
	- plasma: 6-10 mg/dL
	- LR: 5.41 mg/dL

[^1]: https://emcrit.org/ibcc/hypercalcemia/#volume_resuscitation
