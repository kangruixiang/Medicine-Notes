# ventilatory threshold is point of max exercise capacity

- related: [[cardiopulmonary exercise test CPET]]
- tags: #permanent #pulmonology
---
- the amount of air being moved ($V_e$) now exceeds the amount of oxygen and CO2 being exchanged ($V_{O_2}$ and $V_{CO_2}$), and this gets worse as work increases. This causes a change in slope of $V_e/V_{O_2}$ and $V_e/V_{CO_2}$

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20240701063909.png)

- this threshold point is typically when $V_{O_2}$ is at 40-60% peak $V_{O_2}$

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20240701063922.png)[^1]

- elevated ventilatory threshold correlates with better athletic training (takes longer to reach anaerobic threshold)
- normal ventilatory threshold could mean poor effort but could also mean diseases
- decreased ventilatory threshold means diseases
