# acute liver failure differs from acute liver injury

- related: [[GI gastroenterology]], [[acute fatty liver of pregnancy]]
- tags: #literature #GI #icu
---

<!-- what are criteria for acute liver failure? How does it differ from acute liver injury? b:1469953908148-->

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/20250731090916.png)

The 3 criteria for acute liver failure are evidence of hepatic injury (eg, elevated aminotransferases), encephalopathy, and INR ≥1.5.

- differentiate from acute liver injury, which do not have these:
	- synthetic dysfunction
	- encephalopathy
	- previously healthy livers
	- can last up to 6 months time[^1]
