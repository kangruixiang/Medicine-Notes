# gabapentin can cause hypercapnic respiratory failure with nyastagmus

- related: [[Pharmacology]]
- tags: #literature #icu
---

<!-- differentiate gabapentin vs opioid in overdose b:1572269537733-->

Because this patient has chronic kidney disease, she is at high risk for adverse reactions from drugs that are cleared by the kidney. Gabapentinoids are antiepileptic drugs commonly used as adjuncts in the treatment of chronic pain. Gabapentinoid-related hypercapnic respiratory failure has been increasingly reported, especially among patients with impaired renal function. This patient's ==dilated pupils== and ==horizontal nystagmus== are also characteristic. The concomitant use of CNS depressants such as opioids, anxiolytics, antidepressants, or antihistamines further increase this risk.

Opioids, including those prescribed for legitimate reasons, are the most common cause of drug overdose death. Approximately 25% of patients who are prescribed opioids will use them inappropriately. This patient is at risk for opioid-induced respiratory depression. However, her lack of response to a large dose of naloxone, her ==midrange pupils==, and her ==horizontal nystagmus== would be unusual features for opioid intoxication.

Isopropyl alcohol is a skin disinfectant, antifreeze, and solvent. Accidental or intentional ingestion resembles ethanol intoxication and can cause deep sedation and coma. Unlike methanol and ethylene glycol, isopropyl alcohol does not increase the anion gap. Significant intoxication does increase the osmolar gap (measured - calculated serum mOsm/kg >10 [mmol/kg >10]) and often stimulates ketogenesis. Serum ketones were not detected, and the osmolar gap was only 1 mOsm/kg (1 mmol/kg).

Metformin is also excreted by the kidneys and can accumulate in patients like this one. Metformin overdose typically causes lactic acidosis without respiratory depression. Other nonspecific signs and symptoms include anorexia, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, increased thirst, abdominal pain, hypotension, hypothermia, and altered mentation.[^1][^2][^3][^4]
