# alveolar gas equation calculates oxygen content of alveoli

- related: [[pathophysiology of hypoxia and hypoxemia]]
- tags: #permanent #pulmonology
---

<!-- what is the alveolar gas equation b:1346415246304-->

Oxygen content of alveoli is dependent on oxygen content of inspired air and mixing of $CO_2$ gas exchange.

$$
P_{AO_2}=P_{IO_2}-\frac{P{aCO_2}}{R} = 150-\frac{P_{aCO_2}}{0.8}
$$

![](https://photos.thisispiggy.com/file/wikiFiles/gzZuud7.jpg)

- $P_{IO_2}$: inspired air O2 content
- $P_{AO_2}$: alveolar O2 content is lower because of gas exchange
- $P_{aO_2}$: arterial blood content is lower because of shunt
- $S_{aO_2}$: oxygen saturation is directly dependent on arterial oxygen content. Oxygen saturation is percentage of hemoglobin site that's bound with oxygen molecule.
